## Superstore Sales Analysis
An exploratory data analysis of a retail superstore dataset to uncover sales and profit trends across regions, categories, states, and shipping modes.

### Dataset
Retail dataset containing sales, profit, category, sub-category, region, state, segment, and shipping mode information.

### Tools
Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn

### Key Insights

**Sales Distribution**
- Sales are highly right-skewed (mean: 229.86, median: 54.49), indicating that a small number of high-value transactions drive overall revenue  
- High standard deviation (623.25) shows significant variability in sales  

**Category & Sub-Category**
- Technology is the most profitable category  
- Furniture generates strong sales but lower profit margins  
- Copiers and Machines are top-performing sub-categories  
- Tables consistently generate losses despite moderate sales  

**Regional Performance**
- West leads in both sales and profit  
- East follows as the second strongest region  
- South is more profitable than Central despite lower sales  

**State Performance**
- California records the highest sales volume  
- Top states: California, New York, Texas, Pennsylvania, Washington  
- New York outperforms California in profit for premium shipping modes  

**Shipping & Segment**
- Standard Class dominates across all regions  
- Sales performance varies across shipping modes, with premium shipping yielding better profitability in some states  


### 📈 Overall
- Profit margin of ~12.5% indicates a profitable business, though losses in specific sub-categories (e.g., Tables) highlight areas for optimisation
